About OFK

Ohlone for Kids and Teens, administrated by Ohlone College, offers a unique blend of academic and special interest enrichment classes for students in grades 4-9. Students will develop confidence in a safe and supportive environment while engaging in hands-on learning activities that stimulate their interest and growth through an interactive, facilitative workshop format. Students practice critical thinking skills and enjoy a variety of activities including Arts/Performing arts, Language Arts, English, Math, Reading, LEGO Robotics, Electronics, Science, Social Studies, and SAT/College Preparation.

Authentic US Domestic Program
with Varieties of Classes

This is a long-standing original summer enrichment program designed for local commuting students in the neighborhood school district. International students can independently choose classes of their interests, learn in the normal American classroom setting, and interact with the domestic students. This is a public program with open enrollment that is different from those typical study-group based that international students are always together for the same classes without mingling with local students.

Hands-on Learning and
Diverse Culture Immersion

Summer is an opportunity to stay away from any traditional instruction based education. Come to experience hands-on and interactive learning to develop your practical skills.

International students can taste the ways American classes are conducted and how American students learn. They will also gain perspectives toward their upcoming high school and college career. International students can experience more diverse culture from this open-community summer program of a public school district than those hosted by private boarding schools under the closed campus environment.

Academic Guidance and
Residential Advisory

Live-in residential counselors from prestige university will provide academic and cultural guidance to international students. This enrichment program is wrapped around the curriculum with value-added services and extra-curriculum activities to cultivate student's leadership, independence and self confidence under yet a fun-packed environment.

The program staffs are educators and parents of young adults themselves that are experienced in managing international programs for over 20 years. Parents should have high confidence in program staffs that their students will be well taken care of.

Academic Value
Residential Program

Housing

  • Residential program operated by CFM Educational Services, a well-recognized partner of UC Berkeley Graduate School of Education and Lawrence Hall of Science and with extensive experience in providing credible international education and reliable student service with many U.S. universities and organizations since 1991
  • Accommodation at CSU East Bay college campus with high-standard apartments giving students a safe, pristine, and academic living experience
  • Live-in residential counselors from prestige universities offering academic advising, cultural guidance, social learning, and safety supervision during daily living and activities

Meals

Three meals a day, designed for balance and variety:

  • Breakfast - Take continental breakfast in the dorm consisting of a few of the following: Muffin, cereal, soymilk/milk, toast, bagel, yogurt, fruits, pancake, waffle etc.
  • Lunch - Experience American student’s typical brown-bag, mostly cold, lunches and have picnic on campus
  • Snacks – Take healthy daily snacks after class and before dinner to keep students energized and productive (Granola Bar, cookies etc.)
  • Dinner - Try authentic food with multi-cultural cuisine, such as Mexican, Italian, American and Chinese foods at local restaurants nearby or dine at CSU-EB university cafeteria
